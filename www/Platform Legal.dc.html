Compliance & responsibilities

What CiaraLink provides as software — and what your organisation must own as an NDIS or healthcare provider.
Not legal advice. This page is a plain-language summary for provider administrators. It does not replace advice from an Australian lawyer, NDIS consultant, payroll specialist, or the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission. CiaraLink has not had these materials reviewed by a lawyer unless your platform operator tells you otherwise.
CiaraLink (platform)

What we operate

  • The SaaS product — hosting, security, updates, support
  • Terms of Service and Privacy Policy for the platform
  • Software tools: rostering, records, credentials, incidents, payroll aids, claiming aids, integrations

We are not your NDIS registered provider and do not deliver care, employ your workers, or submit claims or SIRS reports on your behalf.

Your organisation (provider)

What you remain responsible for

  • NDIS registration (where required) and Practice Standards
  • Worker screening and credential validity
  • Participant consent and care quality
  • Accurate payroll, tax, super and STP
  • Correct NDIA claims and evidence
  • SIRS reporting to the NDIS Commission

Before you rely on payroll or claiming

SCHADS rates and NDIS Price Guide figures in CiaraLink may be placeholders until verified by qualified consultants. Always review calculations and claim lines before paying staff or submitting to the NDIA.

Privacy roles

For participant and worker records your team enters, your organisation is typically the privacy controller. CiaraLink processes that data on your instructions to run the software. Direct privacy questions about care records to your organisation first; platform questions to CiaraLink via the contact on our website.
